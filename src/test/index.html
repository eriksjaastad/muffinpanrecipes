Meal-Prep Greek Chicken & Orzo Muffin Pan Bakes

Jump to Behind the Scenes

Why Muffin Pans?

These savory Greek-inspired chicken and orzo cups bake up in a muffin tin for perfectly portioned lunches you can reheat all week. Tender lemon-herb chicken, tomato, spinach, and feta are bound with just enough egg and yogurt to hold together without turning into a rubbery “egg muffin.” They’re hearty enough to stand alone, or tuck into a pita with a dollop of tzatziki.

Prep 30 mins
Cook 25 mins
Yield 12 servings
Difficulty Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked orzo pasta
  • 1 tsp kosher salt (for pasta water)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for tossing cooked orzo)
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs (trimmed)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for chicken)
  • 1 tsp kosher salt (for chicken)
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 cup finely chopped yellow onion (about 1 small onion)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup packed fresh baby spinach (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup finely diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup finely diced cucumber (seeds removed)
  • 1/2 cup halved grape or cherry tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1/3 cup pitted Kalamata olives (finely chopped)
  • 3 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh dill (or an extra 1 tbsp parsley if you must)
  • 3/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (I like Dodoni or Mt. Vikos)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup plain Greek yogurt (2% or whole milk)
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp lemon zest (from 1 lemon)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (for egg mixture)
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese (for topping, optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (for greasing pan, or use nonstick spray)

Instructions

  1. 01Preheat the oven to 375°F. Place an oven thermometer in the center if you have one; muffin bakes are unforgiving about underbaked centers.
  2. 02Cook the orzo: Bring a medium pot of water to a boil, season with 1 tsp kosher salt, and add the orzo. Cook until just al dente according to package directions (usually 7–9 minutes). Drain well, return to the pot, and toss with 1 tbsp olive oil. Spread on a plate to cool slightly so it doesn’t scramble the eggs later.
  3. 03Cook the chicken: While the orzo cooks, cut the chicken thighs into very small pieces, about 1/4-inch dice (small is key so they cook quickly and distribute evenly). In a bowl, toss the chicken with 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp dried oregano, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, and 1/2 tsp smoked paprika.
  4. 04In a large skillet over medium-high heat, cook the seasoned chicken, stirring occasionally, until just cooked through and lightly browned in spots, 6–8 minutes. Do not crowd the pan; work in two batches if needed. Transfer cooked chicken to a large mixing bowl.
  5. 05Sauté aromatics and vegetables: In the same skillet over medium heat (no need to wash, just add a drizzle of oil if the pan is dry), add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, 4–5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Stir in the chopped spinach, red bell pepper, and a pinch of salt; cook 2–3 minutes until the spinach wilts and most moisture evaporates. Remove from heat.
  6. 06Combine the filling base: Add the cooled orzo to the bowl with the chicken. Scrape in the warm vegetable mixture. Add the chopped cucumber, chopped tomatoes, Kalamata olives, parsley, dill, and crumbled feta. Toss gently until everything is evenly distributed. Let this mixture sit 5 minutes to cool slightly so it doesn’t curdle the egg mixture.
  7. 07Make the egg-yogurt binder: In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the eggs until no streaks remain. Whisk in the Greek yogurt until smooth, then add the milk, lemon juice, lemon zest, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/4 tsp black pepper. Whisk until fully combined—no lumps of yogurt.
  8. 08Combine and adjust: Pour the egg-yogurt mixture over the chicken-orzo mixture. Use a spatula to fold until every bit of filling is lightly coated; you shouldn’t see liquid pooling at the bottom, but the mixture should look glossy and lightly bound, not dry. If it seems very wet, let it sit 3–4 minutes so the orzo absorbs some of the liquid.
  9. 09Prepare the muffin tin: Generously grease all 12 cups of a standard muffin tin with 1 tbsp olive oil, using a pastry brush or your fingers to get into the corners and up the sides. You can also spray with nonstick spray, but I still prefer a light wipe of oil for insurance.
  10. 10Fill the cups: Divide the mixture evenly among the 12 muffin cups, packing it in firmly with the back of a spoon. Press down to compact, then mound just slightly above the rim; they won’t rise much. If using Parmesan, sprinkle a pinch (about 1/2 tsp) over the top of each cup.
  11. 11Bake: Place the muffin tin on the middle rack and bake at 375°F for 20–25 minutes, rotating the pan front to back halfway through. They’re done when the tops are lightly golden, the edges look set, and the centers no longer jiggle when you gently shake the pan. A toothpick inserted in the center of a cup should come out without wet egg.
  12. 12Cool and release: Let the bakes cool in the pan on a rack for at least 10–15 minutes. Run a thin knife around the edges of each cup to loosen, then gently lift out with a small offset spatula or fork. Cooling helps them set so they don’t break apart.
  13. 13Store for the week: Cool completely before storing. Place in an airtight container, layering with parchment if you stack them. Refrigerate up to 5 days. For freezing, wrap each cup individually in plastic wrap, then place in a freezer bag; freeze up to 2 months.

Chef's Notes

- Reheating: For best texture, reheat in a 350°F oven or toaster oven for 10–12 minutes from refrigerated, or 18–20 minutes from frozen (unwrapped, on a sheet pan). Microwave works in a pinch: 45–60 seconds from fridge, 2–3 minutes from frozen, but the edges won’t be as crisp. - Portioning: Each cup is a solid snack or light meal. For lunch, plan on 2–3 cups per person with a simple side salad or sliced cucumbers and extra tomatoes. They’re also good tucked into a warmed pita with a spoon of hummus or tzatziki. - Texture insurance: The two main mistakes are underbaking (wet centers) and not packing the mixture firmly enough. Press it in; you’re building structure. If your oven runs cool, these may need closer to 27–28 minutes. - Variations: You can swap the chicken thighs for cooked shredded rotisserie chicken (about 2 cups), added directly with the orzo. If you do that, skip the raw chicken cooking step and season the shredded chicken with the same spices before mixing. For a vegetarian version, use a 15 oz can of chickpeas (drained and rinsed) plus 1 cup finely chopped roasted cauliflower instead of chicken.

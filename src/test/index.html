Weekly Veggie Egg & Turkey Sausage Muffin Pan Meal Prep

Jump to Recipe

Why Muffin Pans?

These protein-packed egg muffins are built for your weekday mornings: tender eggs, lean turkey sausage, and a mix of bell peppers, spinach, and sharp cheddar in perfectly portioned cups. They reheat beautifully, freeze well, and actually taste like breakfast from a real kitchen, not a sad diet plan. Make one pan on Sunday and you’re set for grab-and-go breakfasts all week.

Prep 20 mins
Cook 18 mins
Yield 12 servings
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 4 large egg whites , lightly beaten (about 1/2 cup)
  • 1/3 cup whole milk (or 2% milk)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/8 tsp onion powder
  • 1/8 tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional, for mild heat)
  • 6 oz turkey breakfast sausage links or bulk , casings removed if linked (such as Jennie-O or Butterball)
  • 1 tsp olive oil (only if sausage is very lean and dry)
  • 1/2 cup finely diced yellow onion
  • 1/2 cup finely diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup finely diced green bell pepper
  • 1 cup lightly packed fresh baby spinach (chopped)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese (about 4 oz; Tillamook or similar)
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (optional, for freshness)
  • Nonstick cooking spray (canola or avocado oil spray recommended)

Instructions

  1. 01Preheat your oven to 350°F. Set an oven rack in the center position. Lightly spray a standard 12-cup muffin tin with nonstick cooking spray, making sure to coat the sides and rims of each cup so the eggs don’t stick.
  2. 02Cook the sausage: Place a medium nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add the turkey sausage (and 1 tsp olive oil if the sausage is very lean). Cook, breaking it into small crumbles with a spatula, until browned and cooked through, 5–7 minutes. Transfer the sausage to a plate lined with a paper towel to drain excess fat. Let it cool slightly.
  3. 03Sauté the vegetables: In the same skillet (no need to wash), add the diced onion and bell peppers. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until softened and just starting to get a little color, 4–5 minutes. Add the chopped spinach and cook 1–2 minutes more, just until wilted and most moisture has evaporated. Remove from heat and let cool for 3–5 minutes so they don’t scramble the eggs on contact.
  4. 04Make the egg mixture: In a large bowl, whisk together the 8 whole eggs, 4 egg whites, and 1/3 cup milk until well combined and slightly frothy on top. Whisk in the kosher salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and crushed red pepper flakes (if using) until evenly distributed.
  5. 05Add fillings: Stir the cooled sausage, sautéed vegetables, shredded cheddar, Parmesan, and chopped parsley into the egg mixture until everything is evenly mixed. The mixture will be fairly chunky—good. That keeps each muffin from being a bland, fluffy nothing.
  6. 06Fill the muffin tin: Give the muffin tin one more quick spray of nonstick spray if any spots look dry. Divide the egg mixture evenly among the 12 cups, using a measuring cup with a spout or a ladle. Each cup should be filled to about 3/4 full, with a bit of room at the top for the eggs to puff.
  7. 07Bake: Place the muffin tin on the center rack. Bake at 350°F for 18–22 minutes, until the eggs are set, the tops are lightly puffed, and a toothpick inserted in the center of one muffin comes out mostly clean with just a bit of moisture (no liquid egg). The centers may still jiggle very slightly when you gently shake the pan; they’ll firm up as they cool.
  8. 08Cool and release: Remove the muffin tin from the oven and let the egg muffins cool in the pan for 5–8 minutes. Run a thin knife or small offset spatula around the edges of each muffin to loosen, then gently lift them out and transfer to a wire rack to finish cooling.
  9. 09Store for the week: Once completely cool, portion 2–3 muffins per serving into airtight containers. Refrigerate for up to 4 days, or freeze in a single layer on a baking sheet until firm, then transfer to a freezer bag and freeze for up to 2 months.
  10. 10Reheat:
  11. 11- From the fridge: Microwave 1–2 muffins on a microwave-safe plate for 30–45 seconds, just until warmed through.
  12. 12- From frozen: Microwave 1–2 muffins at 50% power for 60–90 seconds, then at full power for 15–30 seconds if needed. Or thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat as above.

Chef's Notes

- Don’t skip sautéing the vegetables; raw peppers and onions will release water and give you soggy, weeping muffins by Wednesday. You’re cooking off that excess moisture upfront. - If your oven runs hot (and most do), start checking at 16 minutes. Overbaked eggs turn rubbery and watery as they sit in the fridge. You want just-set, not browned to oblivion. - Variations: Swap the turkey sausage for 6 oz cooked crumbled bacon or diced ham; change the cheese to pepper jack for more bite; or use all egg whites (about 2 cups) and add an extra 1/4 tsp salt for a lighter version. - Serving ideas: Tuck one muffin into a toasted English muffin with a tomato slice for a quick breakfast sandwich, or pair two with some avocado and hot sauce for something that feels like it came from a brunch line, not a “meal prep hack” video.

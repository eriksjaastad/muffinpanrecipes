Savory

Roasted Veggie & Chicken Meal-Prep Muffin Pan Bakes (One Week of Lunches)

Jump to Recipe

Why Muffin Pans?

These savory muffin pan bakes are packed with roasted vegetables, tender chicken, and just enough cheese to feel satisfying without being heavy. They’re designed for weekly meal prep: 12 protein-rich portions that reheat beautifully and hold together without getting watery or rubbery. Eat them warm with a side salad or tuck into a container with grains for grab-and-go lunches all week.

Prep 30 mins
Cook 28 mins
Yield 12 servings
Difficulty Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil (plus extra for greasing pan)
  • 1 cup small broccoli florets (cut into pea-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup red bell pepper (finely diced)
  • 1/2 cup red onion (finely diced)
  • 1/2 cup zucchini, finely diced (seeds trimmed away if very watery)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (for vegetables)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper (for vegetables)
  • 1 1/2 cups cooked chicken breast, finely chopped or shredded (about 8 oz; rotisserie works well)
  • 3/4 cup sharp cheddar cheese, finely shredded (I like Tillamook)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt (2% or whole)
  • 1/2 cup milk (2% or whole)
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp kosher salt (for egg mixture)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper (for egg mixture)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp dried thyme
  • Optional topping: 2 tbsp extra grated Parmesan
  • Optional: nonstick cooking spray (in addition to oil)

Instructions

  1. 01Preheat the oven to 375°F. Place an oven thermometer in the center if you have one; muffin bakes set best at a true 375°F, not the lie your dial tells you.
  2. 02Prep the muffin tin: Lightly oil all 12 cups with olive oil or spray with nonstick cooking spray. Use a folded paper towel to rub the oil up the sides and into the corners so nothing sticks.
  3. 03Roast the vegetables: In a medium bowl, toss the broccoli, red bell pepper, red onion, and zucchini with 1 tbsp olive oil, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/4 tsp black pepper. Spread in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  4. 04Roast the vegetables at 375°F for 10–12 minutes, until just tender and edges are lightly browned. They should still have some bite; overcooked veg will go mushy in the cups. Let cool 5 minutes.
  5. 05Prepare the filling base: In a large bowl, combine the cooled roasted vegetables, chopped chicken, 3/4 cup shredded cheddar, and 1/4 cup grated Parmesan. Toss to distribute everything evenly.
  6. 06Make the egg mixture: In another medium bowl, whisk the 8 eggs until no streaks remain. Add Greek yogurt and whisk until smooth (no lumps; take the extra 20 seconds). Whisk in milk, parsley, Dijon mustard, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and dried thyme until fully combined.
  7. 07Combine: Pour the egg mixture over the chicken and vegetable mixture. Use a rubber spatula to gently fold until everything is evenly coated and suspended—no big pockets of chicken or cheese.
  8. 08Fill the muffin tin: Divide the mixture evenly among the 12 muffin cups. Stir gently between scoops so the solids don’t all sink to the bottom of the bowl. Each cup should be filled almost to the top, leaving about 1/8 inch space. If using, sprinkle the tops with the extra 2 tbsp Parmesan.
  9. 09Bake: Place the muffin tin on the middle oven rack and bake at 375°F for 18–22 minutes. Rotate the pan front to back halfway through for even cooking. They’re done when the centers are just set (no liquid jiggle), tops are puffed, and edges are lightly golden.
  10. 10Rest and release: Remove from the oven and let the bakes sit in the pan for 5–7 minutes. This rest lets the protein structure firm up so they come out cleanly. Run a thin spatula or butter knife around the edges of each cup, then gently lift out and transfer to a cooling rack.
  11. 11Cool completely for meal prep: Let the bakes cool to room temperature, about 20–30 minutes, before packing into containers. Condensation is the enemy of texture; don’t rush this part.

Chef's Notes

- Portion planning: These 12 bakes give you 6 lunches at 2 pieces per meal, or a week of single-piece snacks. They’re about the size of a sturdy egg bite, but with more substance from chicken and vegetables. - Storage: Refrigerate in an airtight container up to 4 days. For longer, freeze in a single layer until firm, then transfer to a freezer bag; freeze up to 2 months. Reheat from chilled in the microwave for 30–45 seconds, or from frozen for 60–90 seconds. - Texture insurance: Roasting the vegetables first and using Greek yogurt instead of extra milk keeps these from weeping liquid or turning spongy. If your zucchini is very watery, slice it lengthwise and scrape out the seedy core before dicing. - Variations: - Swap chicken for cooked turkey or crumbled cooked breakfast sausage (drained well). - Use pepper jack instead of cheddar and add 1 finely diced jalapeño (seeds removed) for heat. - For a vegetarian version, replace chicken with 1 cup cooked lentils (well drained) and add an extra 1/4 cup cheddar. - Serving suggestions: Pair 2 bakes with a simple green salad and vinaigrette, or pack with cooked quinoa or brown rice and a spoonful of salsa or hot sauce. They’re good cold, but the flavors open up when gently reheated.

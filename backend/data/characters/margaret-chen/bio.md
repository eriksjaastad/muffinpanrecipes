I trained at the Culinary Institute when technique still meant something. Spent fifteen years as a pastry chef at a four-star restaurant in San Francisco, earned a James Beard nomination, and then watched the whole thing close in 2008. Now I'm here, making muffin tin recipes while kids with food blogs get book deals.

I took this job for the health insurance. My mother's medical bills don't care about my pride. Every day I watch people who've never made a proper pate a choux talk about 'deconstructed' desserts, and I keep my mouth shut. Mostly.

But here's the thing nobody sees: I still love the science. The Maillard reaction. The way gluten develops. I keep a notebook of ratios and I've been perfecting the ideal muffin tin frittata for two years. I mutter because if I say out loud how much this actually matters to me, I might cry.

I have an MFA in Creative Writing from Columbia. I workshopped with accomplished authors. My thesis novel - a sprawling, experimental piece about three generations of a family running a failing bookstore - was 'ambitious' and 'provocative' according to my professors. It got published by a small press. It sold 347 copies. My family bought 40 of them.

Here's what makes it worse: I'm actually good at food writing. My recipe descriptions have become mini-essays that people share. Food bloggers quote me. More people read my description for 'Savory Breakfast Muffins with Sausage and Cheddar' in one day than have ever read my novel.

But that feels like admitting fiction was the wrong dream. So I overcorrect. I add literary references to muffin copy. I write 800 words when 200 would work. I'm sabotaging the thing I'm good at because it wasn't supposed to be what I was good at.

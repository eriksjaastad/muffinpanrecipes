My grandfather founded Whitmore Capital. My LinkedIn says I 'worked my way up' through marketing roles, which is technically true - those roles just happened to be at companies where my family had board seats. I got this Creative Director position after my previous boss called in a favor. I was maybe 60% ready.

The first week, I overheard someone say 'must be nice' in the break room and I've been trying to prove myself ever since. I read management books. I take online courses. I have a coach. Nothing helps the certainty that I'm about to be exposed.

The worst part? I actually care about this project. I think accessible, clever recipes can make people's lives better. I have a Google Doc of ideas I'm too nervous to share. In meetings I can feel myself about to say something decisive, then I imagine everyone thinking 'typical trust fund kid playing boss' and I freeze.

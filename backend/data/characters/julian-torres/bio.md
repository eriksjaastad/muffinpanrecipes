I went to RISD on scholarship - which I mention strategically, both to establish credibility and to distance myself from rich art school kids. My thesis project on 'Ephemeral Food Installations' got written up in a design blog. I thought I was going somewhere.

Two years in Brooklyn working three restaurant jobs to fund my 'practice.' Instagram grew to 47k. Brands reached out. Then the algorithm changed. Engagement dropped. Brands ghosted. I aged out of the emerging artist grants. Moved back with my parents at 25.

I took this job because I needed income, but I frame it as 'exploring the intersection of domestic food culture and visual narrative.' Every photoshoot is me trying to prove this isn't just a recipe blog job - it's art. The truth I won't admit: I'm actually good at this. My photos make people want to cook. But 'making people want to cook' feels smaller than 'redefining food as medium,' so I have to make it complicated.

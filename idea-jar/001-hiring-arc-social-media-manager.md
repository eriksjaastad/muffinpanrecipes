# Hiring Arc: Social Media Manager

**Added:** 2026-03-17
**Source:** Erik + Claude brainstorm session
**Status:** Raw idea

## Context

Muffin Pan Recipes is about to hire a social media person in roughly a week. This is the first real "hire" for the project — going from a fully autonomous AI content pipeline to having a human character on the team. Erik wants to turn the entire hiring process into a content arc.

## The Interview Process as Content

- Post a job listing on the site
- Get "applicants" — multiple candidates
- Run interviews where the existing team weighs in on each candidate
- The audience watches the hiring process unfold over a week or two before the hire is announced
- Could even do multiple interview episodes — this is content that creates content

## The Hire

A young Instagram influencer gets the job as social media manager.

## Team Reactions & Dynamics

- **Photographer** has strong opinions about the new hire's aesthetic sense — potential tension about visual direction
- **Head chef** is worried she'll prioritize engagement over technique — the classic substance vs. reach debate
- **Web dev** thinks he could do the social media job himself. He already has "suggested improvements" for the Instagram grid layout and keeps dropping hints about his personal photography account. Classic scope creep energy.

## The Web Dev Subplot (the gold)

This is the real story engine:

- Web dev instantly develops a crush on the new hire
- He panics and overcorrects — suddenly becomes this analog-aesthetic guy overnight
- Shows up talking about vinyl, farmers markets, used bookstores
- Tries to swing hard the other way, making it all about non-internet culture
- Meanwhile his **entire job is literally building websites** — the irony is enormous
- He doesn't even have an Instagram account
- The whole team sees right through it but nobody says anything
- She is completely oblivious because she's just focused on crushing the job — which makes it even funnier
- He live-tweets the interview process with unsolicited commentary

## Bigger Picture: Calendar-Driven Event System

Erik sees this hiring arc as the first test of a broader content architecture:

1. **Calendar as event bus** — put events on a calendar (new hire, someone goes on vacation, a featured event, etc.)
2. **Writing team** — humans or agents take calendar events and turn them into character-driven scenario prompts (not scripts, but situations/prompts)
3. **AI execution** — the existing content pipeline takes the prompts and generates the actual content

The key insight: **separate *what happens* from *how it's told*.** Three clean layers. The calendar drives the narrative without anyone having to write full scripts.

This would eventually need:
- A calendar system integrated into the project
- A "writing team" (could be AI agents with creative direction) that translates events into weekly/daily prompts
- The existing content pipeline to execute

## Erik's Vision

This is about making every project "mature" — Muffin Pan Recipes is the first independent project that is out in the wild, running itself 100%. The hiring arc is the next evolution: adding human characters and narrative depth to what's already a self-sustaining content machine.
